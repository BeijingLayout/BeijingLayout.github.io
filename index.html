Innovation Contest

Imagine you are a college student planning an exchange program for your third year. One major challenge in this process is not only selecting your top-choice universities, but also navigating the numerous variables involved. These include your living budget, location preferences, language barriers, testing requirements, and course regulations.

However, one of the most frustrating aspects of the exchange process is course selection. Courses differ across universities in terms of class codes, prerequisites, content coverage, and even naming conventions. This makes it difficult to determine whether courses are truly equivalent.

An additional complication is that even if you believe you have successfully matched courses, there is no guarantee that your home and host universities will approve them as equivalents. Formal approval from your faculty is required, and this process can take several weeks. As a result, you face both a lack of clear information on course compatibility and the risk of not receiving approval in time—potentially just days before your exchange begins.
